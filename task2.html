The Moon

A rocky, differentiated body with a small core, mantle, and crust, covered in impact craters, volcanic plains (maria), and bright highlands, all blanketed in fine dust (regolith) with water ice in shadowed areas, lacking a thick atmosphere

The Venus

Known for its scorching heat (hottest in the solar system due to a thick CO2 atmosphere), slow, backward rotation (a day is longer than a year), crushing atmospheric pressure, and similarities to Earth in size and mass, but with extreme conditions making it inhospitable, lacking moons or rings, and known as the brightest "morning/evening star"

The Neptune

A dark, cold ice giant, the farthest planet from the Sun, known for its deep blue color from methane in its hydrogen/helium atmosphere, supersonic winds (fastest in the solar system), large storms like the Great Dark Spot, and a system of faint rings and at least 14 moons, including Triton.

The Pluto

Diverse dwarf planet with mountains, valleys, plains, and glaciers of frozen nitrogen, methane, and carbon monoxide, famous for its heart-shaped region (Sputnik Planitia) and thin blue atmosphere. It has a rocky core, icy mantle, and potentially a subsurface ocean, along with five moons (Charon, Nix, Hydra, Kerberos and Styx.)
